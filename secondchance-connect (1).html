The problem
The system releases people — but doesn't help them land

Returning citizens face a maze of disconnected services, stigma, and bureaucracy at the exact moment they are most vulnerable.

No stable housing

Most people are released with nowhere to go. Halfway houses are full, landlords reject applications, and families are strained. Homelessness dramatically increases reoffense risk.

Employment barriers

Background checks eliminate candidates before interviews. Most job boards don't filter for fair-chance employers. Skills developed inside go unrecognized on the outside.

Healthcare gaps

Medicaid lapses during incarceration. Mental health and substance use treatment — critical for reentry — are hard to find and unaffordable without coverage.

Information overload

Resources exist but are scattered across government websites, nonprofit directories, and word-of-mouth. Finding the right program at the right time is nearly impossible alone.

How it works
From release day to stability — we're with you every step

SecondChance Connect meets people where they are and connects them to what they need, fast.

Step 01

Tell us your situation

Answer a few simple questions — your state, needs, release date. No signup required. Your privacy is protected.

Step 02

Get matched instantly

We surface programs that fit your exact situation — fair-chance jobs, housing with available beds, clinics that take your insurance.

Step 03

Connect directly

Get phone numbers, addresses, eligibility, and application guidance in plain language — no jargon, no barriers.

Step 04

Track your progress

Save resources, set appointment reminders, and check off milestones as you build stability. A dashboard, not a case file.

Our vision
What this becomes with your support

This prototype proves the concept. With funding, we build the full platform — and change the trajectory of hundreds of thousands of lives.

The north star: no one leaves prison without a plan

We envision SecondChance Connect integrated with state correctional systems so every person receives a personalized reentry resource plan before their release date — housing secured, benefits enrolled, job interviews scheduled. The platform lives in every prison library and on every case manager's screen.

AI-powered matching

A conversational assistant that asks simple questions and surfaces hyper-relevant, real-time resources — no forms, no jargon. Available in English, Spanish, and more.

Live data, verified listings

A network of nonprofit partners and government agencies keeping listings current — beds available, waitlist lengths, and application status updated in real time.

Employer marketplace

A curated, opt-in marketplace of fair-chance employers actively hiring — matched to candidates by skills, location, and transportation access.

Family reconnection tools

Resources for children, spouses, and parents — because stable reentry is a family challenge, not just an individual one.
