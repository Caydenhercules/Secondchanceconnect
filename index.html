The problem
Baltimore has resources — but they're impossible to find

Returning citizens face a maze of disconnected services, transportation barriers, and bureaucratic mazes at the exact moment they need help most.

No stable housing on day 1

Baltimore's halfway houses have long waitlists. No address means no job applications, no benefits, no ID renewal — and much higher risk of returning to incarceration.

Employment barriers

Background checks eliminate candidates before interviews. Fair-chance hiring exists in Baltimore but is hard to find. City construction and healthcare jobs are available but require navigation.

Healthcare gaps

Maryland Medicaid covers returning citizens but enrollment is confusing. Behavioral health services at Chase Brexton, BHSB, and BCRI are underutilized by those who need them most.

Transportation is a lifeline

Without a car, getting to interviews, probation, and clinics depends entirely on MTA buses. Many returning citizens don't know which routes to take or that free CharmPasses are available.

How to get a free MTA CharmPass in Baltimore
Several reentry programs provide free or reduced-fare passes — here's exactly where to go
1. Baltimore City Re-Entry Office
Walk in and tell them you're recently released. They'll issue a transit pass or connect you to a program that will.
📍 21 S. Eutaw St · 📞 410-396-9393
2. Mayor's Office of Employment Development
When you enroll in employment services, ask specifically about transit assistance. They provide CharmPasses for job training and interviews.
📍 417 E. Fayette St · 📞 410-396-1910
3. Goodwill Reentry Employment
Transit passes available to active clients. Enroll in their job training program and ask your case manager for transit assistance.
📍 3600 Belair Rd · 📞 410-234-0443
4. Bon Secours Community Works
Transit support provided to housing and reentry clients. Ask your case manager about CharmPass assistance.
📍 26 N. Fulton Ave · 📞 410-362-3300
5. MCVET (Veterans only)
Transit passes provided as part of wraparound veterans reentry services. Ask your case manager on intake.
📍 301 N. High St · 📞 410-576-9626
6. MTA Reduced Fare Program
If you have a disability or low income, you may qualify for a permanent reduced-fare CharmCard directly from MTA.
📞 410-539-5000 · mta.maryland.gov
